# Prem K. Reddy

Postdoctoral Associate in Chemical Engineering at MIT.

This repository hosts my personal academic website, where I share my research, publications, teaching, and professional work.

## About Me

I work at the intersection of crystallization science, molecular modeling, and materials synthesis. My research combines theory and experiment to study the dynamic processes that govern crystallization in small molecules, metal-organic frameworks, and porous organic materials.

My work has included molecular dynamics, kinetic and population balance modeling, DFT-supported analysis, and experimental techniques such as in-situ GIWAXS, SAXS, EXAFS, wet milling, and materials synthesis. I am especially interested in connecting mechanistic understanding with practical process and materials design.

## Current Role

I am currently a Postdoctoral Associate in Chemical Engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), working with Prof. Allan Myerson.

## Background

Before joining MIT, I completed my PhD in Chemical Engineering at the University of Illinois Chicago, where I worked with Prof. Meenesh Singh. My earlier training includes an MTech in Chemical Engineering from IIT Madras and a BTech in Chemical Engineering from Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University.

My experience spans academic research, teaching, and industry, including process engineering work at AbbVie and research on electrochemical and lab-on-chip detection systems at IIT Madras.

## Research Areas

- Crystallization mechanisms of small molecules
- Metal-organic framework and covalent organic framework synthesis
- Molecular dynamics and microkinetic modeling
- Population balance modeling for particle breakage
- In-situ X-ray scattering and spectroscopy
- Process modeling for pharmaceutical and materials systems
